Logistic Map

1. Introduction

The logistic map is a classic example of how simple nonlinear equations can produce incredibly complex and chaotic behavior. It is defined by the recurrence relation:

\[ x_{n+1} = r x_n (1 - x_n) \]

where \( x \in (0, 1) \) and \( r \in [0, 4] \) is a parameter controlling the system's behavior.

2. Bifurcation Diagram

As the parameter \( r \) increases, the behavior of \( x_n \) changes dramatically:

  • For small \( r \), \( x_n \) converges to a fixed point.
  • As \( r \) increases, the system undergoes period doubling: 2, 4, 8, etc.
  • Eventually, it becomes chaotic, showing no clear pattern.
The bifurcation diagram plots the long-term values of \( x_n \) as a function of \( r \), revealing this complex structure.

4. Applications

The logistic map models population dynamics in ecology but is also important in chaos theory, cryptography, and dynamical systems. It shows how unpredictability can arise from seemingly predictable systems.
