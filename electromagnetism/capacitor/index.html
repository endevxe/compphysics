Capacitor Charging & Discharging in an RC Circuit

1. Overview

An RC circuit consists of a resistor (R) and a capacitor (C) connected in series. When a voltage source is applied, the capacitor charges through the resistor, and when the source is removed, it discharges. The behavior of the voltage across the capacitor is characterized by the time constant:

\[ \tau = RC \]

2. Charging Equation

The voltage across the capacitor when charging is given by:

\[ V_{\text{charge}}(t) = V_0 \left(1 - e^{-t/\tau} \right) \]

At time \( t = \tau \), the voltage reaches about 63% of the maximum value \( V_0 \).

3. Discharging Equation

When the source is removed, the capacitor discharges following:

\[ V_{\text{discharge}}(t) = V_0 e^{-t/\tau} \]

After one time constant, the voltage falls to about 37% of its initial value.
