Electric Field Line Visualization

1. Introduction

Electric field lines are a visual representation of the direction and strength of electric fields generated by electric charges. They provide an intuitive way to understand how positive and negative charges interact with their surroundings.

2. Mathematical Foundation

The electric field \( \vec{E} \) at a point in space due to a point charge \( q \) is given by:

$$ \vec{E} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2} \hat{r} $$

where \( r \) is the distance from the charge, \( \hat{r} \) is the unit vector in the radial direction, and \( \varepsilon_0 \) is the vacuum permittivity. For multiple charges, the net electric field is the vector sum of the individual fields.

3. Simulation Approach

We simulate the electric field by calculating the vector field on a 2D grid due to a configuration of point charges. Field lines are visualized using stream plots, which trace the path a test charge would follow under the influence of the field.

4. Vector Field and Stream Plots

Using libraries like NumPy and Matplotlib in Python, we:

  • Define a grid of points
  • Calculate the electric field vector at each point
  • Plot the field using quiver (for arrows) or streamplot (for smooth field lines)

Streamlines are particularly helpful for visualizing how a test charge would move in the presence of multiple charges, revealing symmetric patterns and null points.

5. Idealized Conditions

Since this is a theoretical model, we assume point charges in vacuum and ignore any boundary effects. No experimental data is required — the fields are derived directly from Coulomb's Law.
