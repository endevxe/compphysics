Ideal Gas Simulation

This project models a collection of particles in a 2D box behaving as an ideal gas. Each particle undergoes elastic collisions with the box walls. The simulation tracks how their velocity distribution evolves, and compares it with the theoretical Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution.

1 · Ideal Gas Assumptions

The simulation assumes:

  • Particles are point-like with no interaction except for collisions.
  • Elastic collisions with container walls conserve energy.
  • No inter-particle collisions (can be added for realism).

2 · Velocity Distribution

As time progresses, even if initialized with arbitrary speeds, the system’s velocity distribution naturally approaches the Maxwell–Boltzmann form: \[ f(v) = \frac{m}{k_B T} v \exp\left( -\frac{m v^2}{2 k_B T} \right) \] In 2D, this form is modified slightly, but the key shape and behavior remains.

3 · Simulation Goals

The animation shows two things:

  1. Particles moving in the box and bouncing off the walls.
  2. A histogram of their speeds over time compared to the Maxwell–Boltzmann curve.

4 · Observations

Over time, the velocity distribution becomes smoother and more bell-shaped. The simulation demonstrates how energy spreads naturally in a system of free particles due to random motion, even without explicit interactions.
