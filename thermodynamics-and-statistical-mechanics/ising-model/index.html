The 2‑D Ising Model

The Ising model captures the essence of ferromagnetism by assigning each lattice site a spin \( s_{i} = \pm 1 \). Spins prefer to align with their nearest neighbours when the coupling \( J > 0 \), competing against thermal fluctuations from temperature \( T \).

1 · Hamiltonian

The energy of a spin configuration is \[ \mathcal{H} = -J \sum_{\langle i,j\rangle} s_i s_j , \] where the sum runs over all nearest‑neighbour pairs on a square lattice and \( J > 0 \) promotes alignment. No external magnetic field is included here.

2 · Metropolis Monte‑Carlo

Exact solutions exist only in two dimensions at zero field, so we generate representative configurations numerically. A Monte‑Carlo sweep chooses a random spin, computes the energy change \( \Delta E \) if it were flipped, and accepts the flip with probability \(\min\!\bigl(1, e^{-\Delta E / k_B T}\bigr)\). Repeating this constitutes a Markov chain sampling the Boltzmann distribution.

3 · Order Parameter

The magnetisation per spin, \[ M = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i s_i , \] serves as the order parameter. Below the critical temperature \( T_c \approx 2.27\,J/k_B \) the system spontaneously picks a majority‑up or majority‑down state and \( |M| \) stays non‑zero. Above \( T_c \) thermal disorder dominates and \( M \) fluctuates around zero.

4 · Phase‑Transition Demonstration

Run the script at a sequence of temperatures. You will observe that the time‑averaged \( |M| \) drops sharply as \( T \) crosses \( T_c \), illustrating a continuous phase transition. Finite‑size effects smear out the sharpness; increasing the lattice size \( L \) sharpens the curve.
